User Experience Sunday - February 12, 2012

Join HourSchool for our first ever User Experience Sunday. Learn front-end technology from the pros in a casual and informal setting and get to know other technologists and designers in Austin! We've bundled together the following five classes, offering students the ability to ramp on interaction design for web and mobile in a short time. UX Sunday is perfect for print and web designers looking to increase their set of design skills, for developers looking to move closer to front-end technologies, or for product and project managers in charge of web, mobile, or other interactive products and teams.

UX Sunday features the following courses and teachers; sign up for one class, or take all five and make a day of it:


Introduction to UX: Personas, Scenarios, and Storyboards

During this class, students will learn the basics of user-centered design - how to focus on the user in order to define products that are usable, useful and desirable. Students will learn how to represent users in a robust and thorough manner, and how to bring these personas to life through written scenarios of use. Then, students will learn how to visualize preliminary scenes and screens - storyboards of the users using your products to achieve their goals. At the end of this class, students will have the ability to move from vague requirements to concept designs, suitable for detailed design or implementation. Sign up here!


Advanced UX: Wireframing and Detailed Design

During this class, students will learn how to expand a concept design through wireframing. Students will explore the use of wireframing as a sketching mechanism for user interface design, and will become familiar with the relationship between UI controls and user goals. At the end of this class, students will have the ability to translate concept design (requirements, use cases, scenarios and storyboards) into cohesive wireframes that illustrate and explore interaction, flow, and information architecture. Sign up here!



Introduction to Visual Design: Color, type, and composition

During this class, students will learn the basics and fundamentals of visual interface design. Students will explore the relationship between color, typography, and composition, and will investigate the relationship between various visual styles and web/mobile sites and conventions. At the end of this class, students will have a basic understanding of visual design principles, and will have gained competency in adding a visual aesthetic and emotion to in-progress interaction designs. Students will acquire photoshop templates suitable for re-use. Sign up here!



Introduction to front-end web technologies: HTML5 and basic css

During this class, students will learn how to code HTML5 and basic CSS (cascading style sheets) in order to bring simple websites to life and augment exsiting wordpress templates and other front-end web tools. Students will learn about the basics of front-end scripting, understand how to use common tags and other scripting paradigms, explore the use of popular HTML templating systems, and produce a simple and functioning website. Sign up here!



Introduction to front-end interactivity: Javascript, jQuery, and advanced CSS

During this class, students will learn how to utilize Javascript and jQuery in order to bring simple scripting to a static website. Students will learn how to create rollovers, basic animations, limited in-page interactivity (suitable for AJAX or other server integration), and how to manipulate objects on the screen based on user interaction and interactivity. At the end of this class, students will be able to bring life to their existing, static websites. Sign up here!
